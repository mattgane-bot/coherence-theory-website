Glossary

Key terms and concepts from Coherence Theory. Each term describes a specific condition, process, or relationship within the framework.

Coherence

The condition under which a system can sustain alignment under constraint without exhausting its means. Not a matter of appearance or style, but a condition of organised endurance through time.

Core

Energy and Entropy

Energy makes organisation possible; entropy continuously degrades structure. All organised systems exist under the condition that organisation must be maintained against decay.

Governing Conditions

The Triad

Gradient, order, and constraint — the general condition under which energy becomes organised into stable structure. Gradient drives change, order organises relationships, constraint stabilises structure.

Governing Conditions

Fundamental Intrinsic Properties

Determine whether structure can exist at all. They govern how matter, force, material, and geometry behave.

Governing Conditions

Complex Intrinsic Properties

Describe the behavioural laws of organised systems through time: emergence, scaling, feedback, thresholds, adaptation, and regulation.

Governing Conditions

Intrinsic Emergence

How structure, centres, and patterns arise from the interaction of energy, constraint, and repeated use. The threshold at which organised structure first appears.

Governing Conditions

The Dynamic Engine

How coherence unfolds and renews through time. Its phases — Translational, Morphogenesis, Emergence, Becoming, Unfolding, and Continuous Improvement — form a recursive sequence.

Process

Centres

Local concentrations of organised relationship through which coherence begins to become legible in space. The primary units from which forms, patterns, and systems develop.

Structure

Congruence

Local integration of strong or compatible centres within a limited area. Occurs when centres reinforce each other within a bounded system.

Evaluation

Wholeness

The condition in which multiple forms of coherence converge into an integrated, surrounding, and mutually reinforcing whole across scales and domains.

Evaluation

Endurance

The capacity of a system to continue through time without losing viability, coherence, or meaning.

Evaluation

Sublimation

The process through which human instinct, emotion, memory, and social behaviour are transformed into symbolic, cultural, and spatial structure.

Human

Human Conditions

Universal environmental situations in which human beings can function: light and shadow, warmth and shelter, prospect and refuge, water, tactile comfort, circadian rhythms.

Human

Neurophysical System

How living organisms perceive, regulate, and respond to their environment through sensory processing, movement, prediction, and autonomic regulation.

Human

Alignment

Compatibility between physical, biological, human, and temporal constraints. When these layers align, systems stabilise and endure.

Evaluation

Pattern Languages

Recurring spatial, social, and organisational relationships that allow systems to work well and endure through time. How coherence is reproduced.

Application

Form Languages

The geometric, material, and spatial means through which coherent relationships are physically expressed and constructed.

Application

Perceptive Emergence

The human recognition and experience of structure as coherent, meaningful, beautiful, or alive. How intrinsic structure becomes legible through perception.

Human

Morphogenesis

The Dynamic Engine phase where centres and forms appear. The first stable structural condition where energy becomes organised into recognisable structure.

Process

Livingness

The degree to which an environment supports, nurtures, and sustains life over time. Includes comfort, health, usability, repair, adaptability, and social function.

Quality

Aliveness

The felt quality of vitality, intensity, and perceptual presence in a place. Recognised as a sense of rightness or presence when a system is deeply coherent.

Quality

Coherence Hierarchy

Evaluative framework across six domains: aesthetic/structural, dynamic, livingness, societal, symbolic, and connection to nature coherence.

Evaluation

Organised Under Constraint

Structure and order form not freely or randomly but are shaped by limits, boundaries, and opposing forces. Without constraint there is no structure, only dispersion.
